What Factors Affect Human Development?

Human Development Index, or HDI for short, is a metric created by the United Nations to assess the progress of nations around the world. Factors that affect a country's HDI include health, education, economic prosperity and quality of life. This metric allows the UN to compare countries with similar economic backgrounds, and asses how and why they can have varying qualities of life.
